SCENARIOS BANK FOR ASSESSMENT

Purpose: These scenarios are used for learner assessment throughout Module 2 and Module 3. Format: Scenarios progress from foundational to complex

How to Use These Scenarios

For Learners:

  • Read each scenario carefully
  • Apply the four-step diagnostic framework (Observe, Isolate, Test, Decide)
  • Write detailed responses
  • Submit to your mentor for feedback

For Mentors:

  • Use the accompanying Mentor Guide for assessment criteria
  • Look for systematic thinking, not just "correct" answers
  • Provide constructive feedback
  • Identify learning opportunities

FOUNDATIONAL SCENARIOS (Module 2)

These scenarios have relatively straightforward diagnoses with one primary issue.

Scenario F1: The Slow Startup

Situation:

Maria, a translation consultant in rural Peru, contacts you saying her notebook takes "forever" to start up now. She says it used to boot in under a minute, but now it takes 5-7 minutes. Once it's running, programs seem to work normally, though sometimes they take a moment to open.

Context:

Maria has been in her location for 18 months. The area experiences frequent power outages, and she doesn't have a surge protector. She works primarily with translation software and document processing.

Your task:

  1. What is most likely causing this problem?
  2. What questions would you ask Maria to confirm your diagnosis?
  3. What steps would you recommend?
  4. What preventive advice would you give?

Scenario F2: The Black Screen

Situation:

James calls you from Papua New Guinea saying his computer "won't turn on." When you ask him to describe exactly what happens, he says: "I press the power button and I can hear the fan running, and there's a small light on the front that comes on, but the screen stays completely black."

Context:

James works in a coastal area with high humidity. This morning he noticed some condensation on his desk when he came into the office.

Your task:

  1. What are the possible causes of this problem?
  2. What diagnostic steps would you walk James through?
  3. Based on the most likely cause, what would you recommend?
  4. What environmental precautions should James consider?

Scenario F3: The Charging Problem

Situation:

Priya in rural India reports that her notebook battery "isn't charging properly." She says the charging light comes on when she plugs it in, but the battery percentage doesn't seem to increase. Sometimes it says "plugged in, not charging" in the system tray. The laptop is about 2 years old.

Context:

Priya's area experiences significant voltage fluctuations. The temperature in her workspace regularly reaches 35-40°C (95-104°F) during the day.

Your task:

  1. What are the likely causes of this issue?
  2. How would you determine if this is a battery problem, power adapter problem, or something else?
  3. What immediate recommendations would you make?
  4. What long-term preventive measures should Priya implement?

INTERMEDIATE SCENARIOS (Module 2)

These scenarios require more diagnostic thinking with some ambiguity.

Scenario I1: The Intermittent Shutdown

Situation:

David in Chad contacts you frustrated because his notebook has shut down unexpectedly three times this week while he was working. He says there's no warning—it just turns off suddenly. When he restarts it, sometimes it works fine for hours, other times it shuts down again within 30 minutes. He mentions that it seems to happen more often when he's running his translation software and has multiple documents open.

Context:

It's dry season, and dust is a constant problem. David works in a small office with no air conditioning. He's been in location for 8 months and hasn't done any maintenance on the computer.

Your task:

  1. What is your primary hypothesis, and what is an alternative possibility?
  2. What specific diagnostic questions or tests would help you distinguish between these possibilities?
  3. What solution would you recommend, and what would you tell David if the problem persists after trying your solution?
  4. What maintenance routine should David establish?

Scenario I2: The Mysterious Beeping

Situation:

Sarah emails you from Mongolia describing a strange problem: "When I turn on my laptop, it makes three beeps and nothing appears on the screen. I've tried turning it off and on several times, but the same thing happens. Yesterday it was working fine."

Context:

Sarah recently traveled from her host city to a remote village location, transporting her notebook in her backpack. The journey involved several hours on rough roads. She works with audio recording equipment and language documentation software.

Your task:

  1. What do the beep codes typically indicate, and what are the most likely specific causes given the context?
  2. What would you ask Sarah to check or try?
  3. If your initial suggestions don't resolve the issue, what would be your next recommendation?
  4. What advice would you give Sarah about transporting her equipment in the future?

Scenario I3: The Performance Puzzle

Situation:

Michael in Indonesia reports that his computer has become "unbearably slow" over the past two weeks. Programs freeze frequently, the cursor sometimes stutters, and he's getting pop-up messages saying "your disk is almost full" even though he's sure he hasn't added that many files. He says the computer also feels hot to the touch on the bottom.

Context:

Michael has been using his notebook for language survey work, collecting audio recordings from speakers. He's in a humid environment and works partly indoors, partly in field locations.

Your task:

  1. There seem to be multiple issues here. How would you prioritize investigating them?
  2. Which of these symptoms might be related to each other, and which might be separate problems?
  3. Walk through your diagnostic approach—what would you check first, second, third?
  4. What solutions would you recommend for each issue you identify?

COMPLEX SCENARIOS (Module 3)

These scenarios involve multiple factors, require judgment calls, and test decision-making skills.

Scenario C1: The Cascade of Problems

Situation:

Lisa in Ethiopia contacts you with a complicated situation. Two days ago, there was a severe thunderstorm and power surge. Her notebook was plugged in at the time (no surge protector). After the storm, the computer seemed to work, but she noticed the battery icon showed "not charging." She continued working on battery power. Yesterday, the computer shut down (battery depleted), and now when she tries to start it, she gets the following symptoms:

  • Power light comes on briefly then goes off
  • She hears a clicking sound from inside the computer
  • Screen remains black
  • Sometimes she smells a faint burning odor

Context:

Lisa is 4 hours from the nearest city where replacement parts might be available. She has critical translation work due in one week. There's another language worker in a nearby village (2 hours away) who might be able to loan equipment.

Your task:

  1. Analyze the multiple symptoms—what components are likely damaged?
  2. Is this something Lisa should attempt to troubleshoot further, or should she pursue other options immediately? Explain your reasoning.
  3. What is your recommended action plan, considering the time constraint and resource limitations?
  4. After this incident is resolved, what systemic changes would you recommend for Lisa's work setup?

Scenario C2: The Gradual Decline

Situation:

Robert in Cameroon describes a pattern that's been developing over several months: His notebook has gradually become less reliable. The timeline he provides:

  • 3 months ago: occasional unexplained restarts
  • 2 months ago: programs started crashing more frequently
  • 1 month ago: display sometimes shows colored lines or flickers
  • This week: computer sometimes doesn't recognize his external hard drive when he plugs it in, and yesterday Windows gave an error message about a "hardware malfunction"

The computer still works, but he's worried about losing data and wants to know if he should request a replacement or if these issues can be resolved.

Context:

Robert works in a hot, dusty environment. He backs up his work to an external drive weekly but hasn't backed up in the past 10 days. The nearest IT support is a 6-hour journey. Requesting and receiving a replacement notebook typically takes 2-3 months.

Your task:

  1. What pattern do you see in these symptoms, and what does it suggest about the underlying problem(s)?
  2. What is the urgency level of this situation—should Robert continue using the computer or take immediate action?
  3. Outline a comprehensive action plan that addresses both immediate concerns and long-term solutions.
  4. How would you help Robert think through the trade-offs of continuing with this computer versus requesting a replacement?

Scenario C3: The Diagnostic Challenge

Situation:

You receive this message from Kenji in Nepal: "My computer is acting strange. Sometimes when I'm typing, the cursor jumps to a different place on the screen, or a window I wasn't clicking on suddenly comes to the front. At first, I thought I was accidentally touching the touchpad, but it happens even when my hands are only on the keyboard. Also, maybe unrelated, but sometimes the computer seems to start doing things on its own—I'll see the cursor moving or programs opening when I'm not touching anything. It's making it really difficult to work."

Context:

Kenji works in a mountainous region with temperature extremes (very cold at night, warm during the day). He mentions that the problems seem worse in the morning when he first starts working. His antivirus software is up to date, and he's careful about what he downloads.

Your task:

  1. This scenario could have hardware or software causes (or both). What are the possible explanations you would consider?
  2. Design a systematic diagnostic process to determine the root cause. What would you have Kenji check or try, and in what order?
  3. Based on the most likely cause, what solution would you recommend?
  4. If your initial diagnosis turns out to be wrong, what would be your alternative hypothesis and approach?

ADDITIONAL PRACTICE SCENARIOS

These scenarios can be used for additional practice or testing.

Practice Scenario P1: The Morning Mystery

Situation:

Chen in a mountainous region of China reports that every morning when she arrives at her office, her computer won't start properly. She says the power button lights up, but the screen stays dark and she hears no sounds from the computer. However, if she waits 30-60 minutes and tries again, it usually starts normally. This has been happening for the past week.

Context:

The office has no heating, and overnight temperatures drop to near freezing. During the day, the office warms up significantly. Chen stores her laptop in a desk drawer overnight.

Your task:

  1. What is the most likely cause of this problem?
  2. What questions would help confirm your diagnosis?
  3. What immediate solution would you recommend?
  4. What long-term preventive measures should Chen implement?

Practice Scenario P2: The Traveling Troubles

Situation:

Ahmed is a traveling language consultant who visits multiple remote locations. He reports that his laptop has been experiencing various issues: sometimes the WiFi stops working, sometimes the USB ports don't recognize devices, and occasionally the screen flickers. The problems seem random and don't follow a consistent pattern. The laptop is 18 months old.

Context:

Ahmed travels frequently by car and motorcycle on rough roads, carrying his laptop in his backpack. He works in various environments from air-conditioned offices to outdoor field locations.

Your task:

  1. What might explain the random, intermittent nature of these various problems?
  2. How would you go about diagnosing this situation?
  3. What recommendations would you make?
  4. What preventive measures would help Ahmed avoid these issues?

Practice Scenario P3: The Data Dilemma

Situation:

Sofia in rural Bolivia contacts you in a panic. Her computer has been making a clicking sound for the past few days, but she continued working because she's on a tight deadline. This morning, the computer started but is extremely slow, and some of her files appear to be corrupted. She can still access the computer, but it freezes frequently. Her last backup was 3 weeks ago.

Context:

Sofia is working on critical language documentation that cannot be replicated - interviews with elderly speakers. She has a project deadline in 3 days. The nearest city with IT support is a full day's travel away.

Your task:

  1. What is happening to Sofia's computer?
  2. What is the urgency level, and what should be Sofia's immediate priority?
  3. What step-by-step action plan would you recommend?
  4. How would you help Sofia prevent this situation in the future?

Practice Scenario P4: The Confusing Crash

Situation:

Marcus reports that his computer has started displaying blue screen errors with different error messages. Sometimes it happens when he's using his translation software, sometimes when browsing the web, and once it happened right after startup. The computer always restarts successfully after the crash. This started about a week ago and is getting more frequent - now happening 2-3 times per day.

Context:

Marcus works in a hot, dry environment. The computer is 2 years old and has been reliable until now. He recently installed some Windows updates.

Your task:

  1. What are the possible causes of this problem? (Consider both hardware and software)
  2. How would you systematically diagnose this issue?
  3. What tests or checks would you recommend, and in what order?
  4. Based on different possible diagnoses, what would your recommendations be?

Using These Scenarios for Assessment

For Foundational Scenarios (F1-F3):

  • Expect learners to identify the primary component issue
  • Look for basic application of the diagnostic framework
  • Assess whether they ask appropriate follow-up questions
  • Check if they consider environmental factors

For Intermediate Scenarios (I1-I3):

  • Expect learners to consider multiple possibilities
  • Look for systematic diagnostic thinking
  • Assess ability to prioritize tests
  • Check if they recognize when to escalate

For Complex Scenarios (C1-C3):

  • Expect learners to handle ambiguity
  • Look for practical judgment about repair vs. replace
  • Assess understanding of resource constraints
  • Check for systems thinking (prevention, long-term planning)

For Practice Scenarios (P1-P4):

  • Use for additional practice or to test specific skills
  • Can be adapted for different learning objectives
  • Good for group discussion or peer learning
